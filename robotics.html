Welcome to my robotics page

In robotics you design, build and program robots. In my opinion programing the robot is the most interesting part and is what I'm going to go into depth on. Programing consist of typing, compiling, and uploading the compiled code to the robot. Code looks like this:

Y1 = (abs(vexRT[Ch3]) > threshold) ? (vexRT[Ch3]) : (0);

Code can be complex and vary mathematical. Now some people say that code is as simple as tying in "go forward" BUT it is not that simple. There is plenty of online "coding" places but none of them are really coding. Coding involves defining what "go forward" is and not just calling it.

Some people say "hey it's a library already made why can't I just use it." Well what if it breaks, well since you can't maintenance it you're screwed. But even if you "have to" use a premade library you should at least know what it all means. As many of my mentors have told me "if you don't know what it means don't use it tell you do." Now some people may think "well if I never use it then how am I supposed to know what it means" well that's not what I meant, what I meant was that do't go and use it in production until you know what it means. Now more people may say "I'm using scratch and it's kept in order." Well yes, but you aren't learning much from that. After some time you have learned everything you can and you need to move on.

That's where I come in. I am really good at helping people make that transition from scratch to something else like C++, java, or 30 other languages.

On this site I am going to walk you through my previous vex robots and the code behind them. Now I and my team are going to do all we can to keep a well-documented website but not all the details will be here so if you have in questions email myps3ylod@gmail.com thanks.
